Matriculation — three lanes working together: Forrest handles the logistics side (forms, payment follow-up, dismissals, records). Chasity handles the academic/IFS side (ensuring no drop in warmth or communication as Fellows enter the program). Angela fills the gaps with special initiatives and community-building that make a non-compulsory program worth showing up for. Marcus keeps Fellows here once they arrive — games, tech, vending, music, and activities that make BRYC a place Fellows choose.
Persistence 101 runs in spring of the senior year and takes the place of Program B in Senior Fellows' weekly programming. It is the primary handoff point between the Senior Team and Persistence.
Jakia and the Senior Team begin gradual engagement in the spring of 11th grade — not a hard handoff, but a ramp-up that ensures Fellows feel continuity as they enter 12th grade.
ACT Boot Camps are open to 9th–12th grade Fellows (and the public on a paid basis). Timing and cohort focus vary by semester.
